A little more about me

Multifaceted

As a son to immigrant parents who were passionate for success and hardworking, I learned from a very young age that if I wanted to be successful and have a purpose I would have to find a job I love. I've found quite a few of these. From working at the Sci-Fi Dine-In Theatre in Disney World and being part of the incredible team there cooking up the best food in the park to currently being a man of many tech hats for the United States Navy. These jobs were very different, and tapped into skills I didnâ€™t even know I had - but in the end, I understood that with passion anything is possible.

Computers have been my life for as long as I can remember. Access to data and endless resources at the touch of your fingertips sounds like absolute magic. As a problem solver by nature, I was drawn towards code. Being able to solve real-world issues and algorithms has been a passion of mine for years. These last 4 years I have taken my passion for computers very seriously and have strived to learn as much as I can.

At the end of my associates I was given a capstone project. With 2 teammates we created an application that could completely eliminate the need for paratroopers to maintain their jump logs on paper. These logs are extremely important, and if lost can cost a paratrooper many of his jumps, and with the very real danger, these high impact jumps can have on paratroopers this is a huge issue. We created an application that complied with the standards set by the Department of Defence and required users to authenticate with their Common Access Cards. This application was a simple form that would input this data into an SQL database. It was simple, fast, convenient, and completely changed how I saw applications. I saw the potential coding has to truly change and impact lives positively. Knowing that gave me an unbelievable drive.

Current Employment

I was very fortunate to have my very first tech job at Atlas Tech. With only an Associates degree and most applicants having a Bachelors, they took a chance on me because of my capstone project. I was hired as a software tester and currently spend most of my time maintaining a web server with various internal web applications, making changes in those web applications to fill a requirement for the team, testing and reporting new versions of software, and resolving technical or networking issues for Subject Matter Experts (SME). This job has allowed me to wear many hats and learn a broad set of skills ranging from full stack development to configuring switches.
